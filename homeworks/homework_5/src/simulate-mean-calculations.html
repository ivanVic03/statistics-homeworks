Arithmetic Mean

The arithmetic mean is the sum of all values divided by their count. Ideal for additive processes, but sensitive to outliers.

Example: {5, 10, 15} → (5 + 10 + 15) / 3 = 10

Geometric Mean

The nth root of the product of positive values. Essential for growth and compounding.

Example: 1.5, 0.5 → √(1.5 × 0.5) − 1 ≈ −0.134

Weighted Mean

Each value is multiplied by its weight and divided by the total of the weights.

Example: 10:100, 40:50 → (1000 + 2000) / 150 = 20

Harmonic Mean

The harmonic mean is ideal for averaging rates or ratios—such as speeds or financial ratios—where smaller values have a stronger influence.

Example: {2, 4, 8} → 3 / (1/2 + 1/4 + 1/8) ≈ 3.429

Quadratic Mean (RMS)

The quadratic mean, or root mean square, measures the magnitude of a set—ideal for values related to energy, power, or signal strength.

Example: {2, 4, 9, 10, 12} → √[(Σx²)/n] ≈ 8.31
