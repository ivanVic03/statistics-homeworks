Beyond the Simple Average

A guide to understanding the arithmetic, geometric, and weighted means.

Arithmetic Mean · Geometric Mean · Weighted Mean · Compare

1. Arithmetic Mean

The sum of all values divided by the number of items. Ideal for additive processes, but sensitive to outliers.

Example: {5, 10, 15} → (5+10+15)/3 = 10

2. Geometric Mean

The nth root of the product of all positive values. Essential for growth rates and compounding effects.

Example: returns +50%, -50% → multipliers: 1.5, 0.5 → √(1.5*0.5)-1 ≈ -0.134

3. Weighted Mean

Each value is multiplied by a weight, summed, and divided by the sum of the weights. Used when some observations matter more than others.

Example: 100 shares at $10, 50 shares at $40 → weighted mean = (1000+2000)/150 = 20

4. Choosing the Right Mean

TypeMain ApplicationConsiderations
ArithmeticAdditive data (e.g. salaries, test scores)Highly sensitive to outliers
GeometricMultiplicative processes (e.g. growth rates)Requires positive values; captures compounding
WeightedDifferent importance or frequency (e.g. portfolios, surveys)Weights must be justified and transparent

Rule of thumb: additive → arithmetic; multiplicative → geometric; variable importance → weighted.

Quick Reference

  • Arithmetic: \(\bar{x} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n x_i\)
  • Geometric: \(G = (\prod_{i=1}^n x_i)^{1/n}\)
  • Weighted: \(\bar{x}_w = \frac{\sum w_i x_i}{\sum w_i}\)

Presentation Tips

  1. Show both formula and numerical example.
  2. Compare results on the same dataset.
  3. Always justify weighting schemes.
