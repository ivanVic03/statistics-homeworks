Analysis of the Foundations of Probability Theory: From Interpretations to Axiomatization

Interpretations of Probability and Their Conceptual Inconsistencies Kolmogorov's Unifying Framework Probability as Measure Theory Formal Derivations from Kolmogorov’s Axioms

Probability theory offers a structured framework to reason about uncertainty and to quantify the likelihood of events in diverse contexts. Over the years, several interpretations of probability have been developed—classical, frequentist, Bayesian, and geometric—each providing valuable insights but also posing potential conceptual ambiguities when viewed in isolation.

The axiomatic approach, pioneered by Kolmogorov, provides a unified and consistent foundation, resolving these ambiguities and allowing for rigorous treatment of probabilistic phenomena. This approach is deeply intertwined with measure theory, introducing key constructs such as sigma-algebras, probability measures, and measurable functions, which together enable a precise definition of random variables.

In this context, we explore the fundamental properties of probability, including subadditivity and the inclusion–exclusion principle, and illustrate how the axiomatic framework ensures clarity and coherence across various interpretations, establishing a solid foundation for advanced studies in probability and statistics.
