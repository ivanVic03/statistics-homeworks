3.1 Measure-Theoretic Context

A probability space \((\Omega, \mathcal{F}, P)\) is a normalized measure space, where \(P(\Omega)=1\).

3.2 The \(\sigma\)-Algebra \(\mathcal{F}\)

A \(\sigma\)-algebra is a family of subsets of \(\Omega\) satisfying:

  • \(\Omega \in \mathcal{F}\)
  • Closed under complements
  • Closed under countable unions

3.3 The Probability Measure \(P\)

The probability measure is a \(\sigma\)-additive function \(P: \mathcal{F} \to [0,1]\).

3.4 Random Variables as Measurable Functions

A random variable is a measurable function:

\[ X : \Omega \to \mathbb{R} \]

Measurability requires:

\[ X^{-1}(B) \in \mathcal{F} \quad \text{for all } B \in \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R}). \]
