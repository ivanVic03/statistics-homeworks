Law Large Numbers Simulation

Trajectories
This chart shows the cumulative number of successes for each trial in one trajectory. Start at zero and see how the number grows with each success. Each trajectory shows a possible outcome of the same number of trials. Notice how the lines fluctuate differently, even though the overall pattern is similar. Use the “Automatic scroll” button to watch all trajectories in sequence automatically. The line moves up whenever a trial succeeds and stays flat when it fails. Over many trials, you can observe how the results fluctuate around the expected probability.
Histogram of Final Successes
This histogram summarizes the final frequency of successes across all trajectories. Each bar represents how many trajectories ended within a certain success range. Taller bars indicate that more trajectories ended with a final success frequency in that range. This demonstrates the Law of Large Numbers in action. As the number of trajectories and trials increases, the histogram starts resembling a bell-shaped curve, showing a normal-like pattern. This visualization helps you understand how repeated random trials tend to stabilize around the expected probability.
